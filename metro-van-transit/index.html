Commuting into Metro Vancouver from the Fraser Valley


So I have been commuting in and out of the Metro Vancouver area since earlier this year and I wanted to share what I've learned.  Commuting using public transit is a great way to save money on gas/fuel, be environmentally friendly, and also reduce the stress and time spent in traffic.

The optimal commute in, which I use regularly, consists of the following steps:
    Driving from home or another location in the Fraser Valley to the Carvolth Exchange at 202nd Street in Langley
    Taking the 555 Express Bus from the Carvolth Exchange to the Braid Skytrain station
    Taking Skytrain into Burnaby, Vancouver, and/or Richmond (Expo, Millenium, and/or Canada Lines)
    Taking a bus and/or walking from a Skytrain station to my destination

I cannot stress enough how important it is to give yourself enough time to reach your destination.  You may be delayed at any point along your travel route (standstill highway, train fault, late bus, construction) so always add extra time to your overall estimate.
